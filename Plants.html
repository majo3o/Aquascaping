1. Amazon Sword (CLICK ON THE IMAGE FOR AN EFFECT) The Amazon Sword plant is a perfect option for a beginner due to their low-maintenance nature and adaptability to various conditions. This plant can reach heights of 20 inches or more, which creates a perfect background for larger tanks or a focal point in smaller aquariums. It is also ideal for fish to lay their eggs on the leaves, and it keeps the tank water filtrated and clean.
2. Java Fern The Java Fern is one of the most popular and used plants for aquariums. It can easily adapt to different water conditions including varied temperatures, light levels and acid or alkaline water which makes it ideal for both beginners and experienced aquarists. This plant has a distinctive leave structure which makes it ideal for fish to refuge on its leaves. It is easy to plant since it easily attaches to rocks or other surfaces in the aquarium having no need to add substrate
3. Water Wisteria This plant is native to Asia, and it is perfect for beginners. It can reach a height of about 31 cm (12 inches). This species helps prevent the formation of algae and is easy to breed. Another perk is that water wisterias are a great place for a fish to hide. They are also very nutritious and won’t harm the fish if they decide to eat them. Water wisteria’s need the basics to survive, such as light (they can survive with fluorescent or incandescent lightning), carbon dioxide and essential nutrients; they should be kept in an aquarium which temperature is between 18-30 °C.
4. Java moss This specie of plant has a great durability, moss can even withstand an aquarium with no light. Java moss tends to attach to different things in the aquarium, such as rocks; since it is a type of moss you will not have to worry about planting it and having it root in the aquarium. Another perk is that it is affordable for everyone. All these elements make this plant perfect for your tank and increase lifespan
